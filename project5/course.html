introduction

Rwanda’s agriculture transformation agenda is based on the implementation of the national flagship agriculture strategy known as Strategic Plan for Agriculture Transformation (PSTA 4). The fiscal year 2020/21 marked the end of the third year of implementation of PSTA 4 and hence it is time to conduct a Mid-Term Evaluation to show the achievements and hurdles encountered in the road travelled so far. The Mid-Term Evaluation will provide greater insight on future implementation of the Strategy in order to ensure that our food system is able to cope with the effects of the COVID19 pandemic and remain resilient to future adverse events. The Ministry of Agriculture and Animal Resources (MINAGRI) will continue to promote the acceleration of agricultural growth, improvement of citizens’ livelihood, increased shared prosperity to farmers, and private sector investment opportunities in the agriculture sector through an array of tailored incentives. Despite the negative consequences of the COVID-19 pandemic on the food systems, the agriculture sector has shown great resilience. In this fiscal year, more efforts were deployed to increase agriculture growth and its transformation from subsistence to commercial-led production. The involvement of youth and women in the sector have contributed significantly to modernization and value addition in different agricultural value chains. With the close of the fiscal year, Rwanda has also made progress in seeds supply self-sufficiency, including maize, soybean and wheat, and has been able to end all subsidies on imported seed. This is one among many other steps towards reaching our goals of national food self-sufficiency and the Zero Hunger SDG by 2030. PSTA 4 sets out ambitious targets to turn around the agriculture sector and transform it into a knowledge-based, value-creating sector that continues to contributes to the national economy and ensures food and nutrition security by 2024. To achieve this, the sector needs to develop new tools, new technologies, and new products with the potential to remove critical production constraints, and to forge new partnerships with regional and international stakeholders in the food systems to reach the goals of PSTA 4. We have full confidence in the long-standing collaboration with our partners and stakeholders. The experience from past years assures us that there is solid resolve among our partners and deep engagement with our stakeholders that will help us move forward quickly and tackle key challenges on our farms, in our value chains, and throughout our food system, thereby unlock Rwanda’s full potential. I continue to count on the continuous collaboration and support from all partners and stakeholders as we move forward together on this agriculture transformation journey.

Training of Trainers on pineapple management and macro-propagation technique

There is a great challenge in obtaining uniform planting materials in large quantity due to low rate of multiplication by conventional methods and lack of high-quality propagules. The need to solve this problem led to the development of pineapple macro-propagation technique in 2008 by RAB Horticulture program. A major advantage of the macro-propagation technique is that it does not require specialized skills. It is also very cost effective and can be

used to produce large scale uniform material in a relatively short period of time. Coffee performance

In FY 2020/21, a total of 71,883 MT of coffee cherries was processed through 313 Coffee Washing Stations (CWS). Coffee export quantities and revenues have been fluctuating over the past years. Compared to last fiscal year, there was a decrease in exported quantities but revenues increased by 1.8% mainly due to increased average prices from $3 per/kg to $3.6 per/kg. The main exported coffee types were fully washed coffee (77.85%), Semi washed (10.7%), Triage (5.08%) and Natural (4.24%).
